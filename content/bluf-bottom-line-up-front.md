# BLUF: Bottom Line Up Front

State the most important thing first. Every message, email, or brief should open with the decision needed, the key finding, or the action requested. Background, context, and reasoning follow.

This is not about being terse. It is about respecting the reader's time and making it easy to route the message: a reader who only skims still knows what matters and what you need from them.

## How to apply

1. **Lead with the ask or the point.** Before writing, ask: if this person reads only the first sentence, what must they know? Write that sentence first.
2. **Put context after, not before.** Background that builds to a conclusion buries the conclusion. Move it behind the main point.
3. **Make the subject line a BLUF too.** In email, the subject should state the outcome or request, not the topic. "Decision needed: launch date" beats "Launch date discussion."
4. **One BLUF per message.** If you need two bottom lines, send two messages.

## Examples

**Before:**
> Hi, I've been looking into the vendor options we discussed. There were a few factors to consider — timeline, cost, and integration complexity. After reviewing all three, I think we should go with Vendor B.

**After:**
> **Recommend Vendor B.** It has the best integration story and fits our timeline. Cost is 10% higher than Vendor A but within budget. Details below.

---

**Email subject before:** `Re: Q3 roadmap`
**Email subject after:** `Approval needed: Q3 roadmap by Friday`

## Connection to async communication

BLUF is especially valuable in async environments where there is no back-and-forth to clarify intent. A message that buries its point forces the recipient to read everything before they can act or reply, adding unnecessary latency. Leading with the bottom line lets people respond faster and with less friction.
